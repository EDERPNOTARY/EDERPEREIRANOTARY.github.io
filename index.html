Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need an appointment for the Danvers office?

No, appointments are generally not required for our Danvers walk-in office (inside Staples). You can visit us during our operating hours for quick notarization. However, we always recommend calling ahead for complex documents or to confirm our immediate availability.

What form of ID is required for notarization?

You must present a valid, unexpired government-issued photo ID (such as a US Driver’s License, Passport, or state ID card) to verify your identity. The ID must match the name on the document exactly.

What is your mobile notary service area?

Our primary mobile service area covers the entire Northshore Region of Massachusetts, including cities and towns like Danvers, Peabody, Salem, Beverly, Lynn, Saugus, and surrounding areas. We are willing to travel—please contact us with your specific location.

Can a Notary Public give me legal advice about my documents?

No. By Massachusetts law, a Notary Public is prohibited from giving legal advice, drafting legal documents, or making interpretations about the content of your documents. We are impartial witnesses responsible only for verifying identities and witnessing signatures.
