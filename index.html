Frequently Asked Questions

Everything You Need to Know

Do I need an appointment for the Danvers office?

Appointments are not generally required for general notary services at our Danvers office (inside Staples). You may walk in during business hours. However, for Apostille or Loan Signings, or to ensure immediate availability, we recommend calling ahead first.

What forms of ID are accepted for notarization?

You must present a valid, unexpired government-issued photo identification (such as a US Driver’s License, Passport, or state ID card) to verify your identity. The name on the ID must exactly match the name on the document being signed.

How far does your mobile service travel?

Our primary service area covers the entire Northshore Region of Massachusetts, including Danvers, Peabody, Salem, Beverly, Lynn, and surrounding cities. Please contact us with your exact address to confirm the travel fee.

Can you give me legal advice about my document?

No. By Massachusetts law, a Notary Public is strictly prohibited from offering legal advice, drafting legal documents, or making interpretations about the content of your documents. We only verify identities and witness signatures.
